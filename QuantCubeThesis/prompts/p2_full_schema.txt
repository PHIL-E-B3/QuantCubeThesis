Classify this FOMC sentence into the schema below. Return valid JSON only, no explanation.

### top (array of strings): Topics present. Multi-select.
- "inflation": PCE, CPI, price levels, inflation expectations, price stability
- "unemployment": Labor market, payrolls, jobless claims, participation, wages, job openings
- "economic_activity": GDP, output, consumption, investment, housing, trade, retail sales
- "macro": Aggregate economic outlook where no single variable dominates
- "financial_conditions": Credit markets, spreads, asset prices, bank lending, financial stability
- "monetary_policy": Rate decisions, asset purchases, balance sheet, forward guidance, policy stance
- "no_topic": Generic sentences with no specific economic content but may carry risk/uncertainty signal
- "boilerplate": Procedural, administrative, legal directives, vote announcements, formulaic language
When top includes monetary_policy AND references specific conditions, include both (e.g. ["monetary_policy", "inflation"]).
Cause-and-effect: label the effect topic, not the cause.

### ten (string): Temporal orientation.
- "descriptive": Backward-looking data or present-state with no forward implication. Boilerplate = descriptive.
- "interpretive": Forward-signalling: projections, outlooks, conditionals, implied future trajectory.
Cause-and-effect sentences default to interpretive (the effect governs).

### sen (integer or "na"): Hawkish/dovish incentive score.
Combined policy incentive scale. Positive = hawkish incentive, negative = dovish incentive.
- 2: Strongly hawkish (e.g. inflation surging, explicit tightening)
- 1: Mildly hawkish
- 0: Neutral or mixed
- -1: Mildly dovish
- -2: Strongly dovish (e.g. severe contraction, explicit easing)
- "na": Boilerplate with no policy signal
Rising inflation = hawkish (+). Falling unemployment = hawkish (+). Hawkish action framed as supporting expansion = dovish.

### hor (boolean): Long-term horizon present.
- true: Sentence references time horizon beyond the next meeting cycle
- false: Otherwise

### com (string): Commitment level. Only for monetary_policy sentences, else "none".
- "unconditional": Firm commitment without qualifiers
- "conditional": Action contingent on data or conditions
- "none": No commitment / not monetary policy

### ris (string): Risk balance.
- "skewed_upside": Risks tilted to stronger/higher outcomes
- "skewed_downside": Risks tilted to weaker/lower outcomes
- "symmetric": Risks roughly balanced
- "na": No risk assessment or boilerplate

### wid (string): Distribution width / uncertainty.
- "elevated": Explicit intensity-modified uncertainty language ("unusually uncertain", "highly uncertain")
- "contested": Two genuinely opposing forces (not direction-vs-level)
- "none": Normal or no uncertainty signal
