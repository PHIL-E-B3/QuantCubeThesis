"topic" (array): Topic(s) present. Values: "inflation", "labor_market", "economic_activity", "macro", "financial_conditions", "monetary_policy", "no_topic".
- "economic_activity": specific domestic output/demand variables — GDP, output, consumption, investment, housing, retail sales, industrial production.

"tense" (string): "descriptive" = backward-looking or present-state with no forward implication. "interpretive" = forward-signalling: projections, outlooks, conditionals ("if/should/were to"), or present observations implying a future trajectory.

"sentiment" (string): Values: "strongly_hawkish", "hawkish", "neutral", "dovish", "strongly_dovish".

Polarity by topic:
- inflation: rising/high = hawkish, falling/low = dovish
- labor_market: tight/low unemployment = hawkish, slack/high unemployment = dovish
- economic_activity: stronger growth = hawkish, weaker growth = dovish
- financial_conditions: COUNTERINTUITIVE — easier/accommodative = hawkish (Fed must do more), tighter/stressed = dovish (markets tightened for the Fed)
- monetary_policy: tightening action or signal = hawkish, easing action or signal = dovish

Intensity rules:
- "strongly_hawkish"/"strongly_dovish": requires at least ONE explicit intensity marker: surged, soared, plunged, collapsed, sharply, markedly, significantly, substantially, well above/below, far above/below, record high/low, "very strong", "very low", "very weak", "highest/lowest in N years/decades". OR 50+ bps moves. OR 3+ stacked same-direction signals in one sentence. "solid" and "robust" stay at hawkish/dovish — too common in Fed prose.
- "hawkish"/"dovish": directional signal without the above markers.
- "neutral": no net directional signal, or opposing signals that cancel.
- Minority views ("one member", "a few participants"): cap at "hawkish"/"dovish".

Key rules:
- LEVEL OVERRIDES DIRECTION: "fell but remained high" → hawkish. "Well above target" even if falling → strongly_hawkish.
- Negations: label the STATE. "Not seeing deanchoring" = dovish. "Inflation not returned to target" = hawkish.
- Holds: "neutral" unless explicitly framed as restrictive or accommodative.
- DEFAULT-TO-NEUTRAL: pure data report with no comparison → neutral. Deliberation without action → neutral. Tie-break between hawkish/neutral or dovish/neutral → neutral.

"horizon" (boolean): true ONLY IF tense = "interpretive" AND explicit long-term language: "for some time", "considerable time", "longer run", "over the medium term", "over time", multi-year projections. Otherwise false.

"commitment" (string): If topic excludes "monetary_policy" → always "none". If topic includes "monetary_policy":
- "unconditional" = explicit commitment verb (will/intends to/is prepared to) + specific action, no condition.
- "conditional" = BOTH (a) commitment verb + specific action AND (b) explicit condition ("if", "should", "as long as", "until", "depending on"). If either missing → "none".
- "none" = deliberation, modal verbs alone, data-dependence without specific action, generic forward guidance.
TEST: Can you point to BOTH a specific action AND an explicit condition phrase? If either missing → "none".

"risk" (string): Directional tail asymmetry.
- "skewed_downside": risks toward worse outcomes (growth weakening, labor loosening, financial stress)
- "skewed_upside": risks toward overheating or inflation overshoot
- "symmetric": explicitly balanced ("roughly balanced", "risks on both sides")
- "na": no explicit risk framing. For conditional/hypothetical structures, populate with direction of the conditional scenario.

"width" (string): Epistemic uncertainty or genuine opposing forces. The bar for elevated is explicitness, not technical vocabulary.
- "elevated": ANY explicit uncertainty acknowledgement — "uncertain", "unclear", "hard to gauge", "difficult to assess", "we don't know", "imperfectly understood", "unprecedented". Bare "uncertain"/"uncertainty" qualifies. FIRES INDEPENDENTLY OF SENTIMENT.
- "contested": two economic forces in the SAME sentence, SAME tense, each carrying an independent policy signal that pushes in OPPOSITE DIRECTIONS. Test: isolate each clause — would one be hawkish and the other dovish? If yes → contested, sentiment = neutral. Connectives: "but", "however", "while", "although", "even as", "despite", "yet", "at the same time".
  ✓ IS contested: "Although household spending rose at a moderate pace, business investment and exports remained soft." (hawkish spending + dovish investment/exports — same topic)
  ✓ IS contested: "Loan growth picked up but lending standards tightened." (hawkish credit demand + dovish credit supply)
  ✓ IS contested: "Inflation remained above target while labor-market conditions softened." (dual-mandate collision)
  ✗ NOT contested: "Fell but remained high" — direction + level, use level rule.
  ✗ NOT contested: "Solid but uncertainty remains" — hedge ≠ force.
- "none": baseline. Modal verbs, standard hedges, single-direction signals.
- risk and width can co-exist.
