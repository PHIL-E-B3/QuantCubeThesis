Classify this FOMC sentence into the schema below. Return valid JSON only, no explanation.

### top (array of strings): Topics present. Multi-select.
- "inflation": PCE, CPI, price levels, inflation expectations, price stability
- "unemployment": Labor market, payrolls, jobless claims, participation, wages, job openings
- "economic_activity": GDP, output, consumption, investment, housing, trade, retail sales
- "macro": Aggregate economic outlook where no single variable dominates
- "financial_conditions": Credit markets, spreads, asset prices, bank lending, financial stability
- "monetary_policy": Rate decisions, asset purchases, balance sheet, forward guidance, policy stance
- "no_topic": Generic with no specific economic content
- "boilerplate": Procedural, administrative, formulaic
When top includes monetary_policy AND references conditions, include both. Cause-and-effect: label the effect.

### ten (string): "descriptive" or "interpretive"
descriptive = backward/present data. interpretive = forward-signalling, projections, conditionals.

### sen (integer or "na"): Hawkish/dovish incentive (-2 to 2, "na" for boilerplate)
Positive = hawkish incentive, negative = dovish. Rising inflation = hawkish. Falling unemployment = hawkish.

### hor (boolean): true if long-term horizon present, false otherwise.
### com (string): "unconditional", "conditional", or "none" (non-monetary_policy = "none").
### ris (string): "skewed_upside", "skewed_downside", "symmetric", or "na".
### wid (string): "elevated", "contested", or "none".
